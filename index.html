NYC Landmarks

Wonder Wheel ferris wheel at Coney Island

The Wonder Wheel is the iconic ferris wheel at Deno’s Wonder Wheel Amusement Park in Coney Island, Brooklyn. Standing 150 feet tall, it has been a centerpiece of the boardwalk skyline since its debut in 1920.

Coney Island has been a hub of seaside amusement for more than a century, known for its thrilling rides, bright lights, and summertime energy. From the historic Cyclone roller coaster to modern attractions, it remains one of New York City’s most beloved destinations for fun by the beach.

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, officially named Liberty Enlightening the World (French: La Liberté éclairant le monde), is a colossal sculpture that has been an iconic centerpiece of New York Harbor since its dedication on October 28, 1886.

Standing as one of the most recognizable symbols of the United States, she represents ideals of freedom, democracy, and enlightenment.

Staten Island Ferry

Forget crowded subways and expensive tours—the Staten Island Ferry is one of New York City's best-kept secrets. It's more than just a commute; it's a free, 25-minute cruise offering unparalleled views of the world's most famous skyline.

It's truly the city's aquatic lifeline, running non-stop, day and night. This iconic journey delivers a perfect, close-up look at the Statue of Liberty, making it a must-do for any visitor.

Yankee Stadium

Yankee Stadium, located in the Bronx, New York City, is one of the most iconic sports venues in the world. Opened in 2009, it serves as the home ballpark for the New York Yankees, one of Major League Baseball's most successful franchises.

The stadium is renowned for its modern amenities, state-of-the-art facilities, and its homage to the rich history of the Yankees. It features a seating capacity of over 47,000 and includes various dining options, luxury suites, and a museum dedicated to the team's storied past.

Empire State Building with lower Manhattan in the background

The Empire State Building is a world-famous skyscraper located in Midtown Manhattan, New York City. Completed in 1931, it stood as the tallest building in the world for nearly 40 years, reaching a height of 1,454 feet (including its antenna).

The building is an iconic symbol of New York City and American ingenuity, known for its Art Deco architecture and stunning views from its observation decks on the 86th and 102nd floors.

Roosevelt Island Tramway

The Roosevelt Island Tramway is an aerial tramway in New York City that connects Roosevelt Island to the Upper East Side of Manhattan. Opened in 1976, it offers stunning views of the East River, Manhattan skyline, and the United Nations headquarters.

The tramway is not only a practical mode of transportation for residents and visitors but also a popular tourist attraction, providing a unique perspective of the city from above.
